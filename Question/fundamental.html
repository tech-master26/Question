Question: Explain the Code of Ethics of BNMC ?

Code and Ethics of BNMC


Clause-1: Treat the patient as an individual and with respect.

Clause-2:Demonstrate sympathetic and sensitive behavior towards the patien.

Clause-3: Maintain the confidentiality and the privacy of the patient at all times.

Clause-4: Serve all patients equally regardless of religion, gender, age, race, nationality or economical class.

Clause-5: Act as the patients/clients advocate in all matters relating to their care.

Clause-6: Respect the legal right of the patients/clients to be informed about their diagnosis, treatment of prognosis.

Clause-7: Provide a safe environment to promote early recovery, prevention of complications and well-being of the patients.

Clause-8: uphold basic standard of nursing and professional care at all time.

Clause-9: Do not undertake any nursing practice without required authority.

Clause-10: Refrain from undertaking any nursing practice for which you do not have the necessary skills.

Clause-11: Refuse to participate in any unethical medical and nursing practic.

Clause-12: use appropriate restraint only in situations where the patients/client is in danger of harming themselves or others.

Clause-13: Do not accept or request money or gifts of any kind from the patient or their family or carriers.

Clause-14: Take responsibility to maintain and extend your own professional knowledge through appropriate professional development and training.

Clause-15: Do not misuse hospital or any property under ones care or control.

Clause-16: Do not knowingly engage in any activities which are likely to bring the profession into disrepute.

Clause-17: Do not engage in any political activities either directly or indirectly in a professional capacity.

Clause-18: Maintain current registration with the BNMC adhere to all its rules and regulations.

Questionn: Explain the routes of administering medication ?
Routes of Drug Administration:
  1. Systemic route
  2. Local route
A. Systemic route:
  1. Enteral or alimentary Route:
    • Sublingual route
    • Oral route
    • Rectal route
      •
  2. Parenteral route (extra-intestinal)
    • Injection
    • Subcutaneous (SC)
    • Intracutaneous (IC)
    • Intraarterial (IA)
    • Intravenous (IV)
    • Intramuscular (IM)
    • Intraarticular (IArt)
    • Intraperitoneal (IP)
    • Intraventricular
    • Intracardiac
    • Intrapleural
      •
  3. Inhalation:
    • As a gas: e.g., Volatile anaesthetic: N2O, Chloroform
    • As an aerosol: e.g., Beta 2 adrenoceptor agonist: Salbutamol (Bronchodilator)
    • As a powder: e.g., Na chromoglycate
      •
B. Local route:

Applied to the localized area and action is confined to that particular area.

  1. Topical application: Ointment, paste, drops, powder, lotions etc.
    • Skin inunction (by rubbing of drug on skin)
    • To mucous membrane of nose, urethra, rectum, anal canal, vagina.
    • To eye and ear.
      •
  2. Iontophoresis:
    In this process the drug is applied to the particular area of skin and Galvanic current is used to increase absorption through skin.

Types of Blood Pressure:

Blood Pressure Description
Systolic blood pressure It is the maximum pressure during systole.
Normal Range: 100-140mm of Hg
Average: 120 mm of Hg
Diastolic pressure It is the minimum pressure during diastole.
Normal Range: 60-90 mm of Hg
Average: 80 mm of Hg
Pulse pressure It is different between systolic and diastolic pressure.
Normal Range: 30-40 mm of Hg
Mean pressure It is the average pressure persist in the circulation
  • Diastolic pressure + one third (1/3) of pulse pressure or
  • Two third of diastolic pressure + one third of systolic pressure.
